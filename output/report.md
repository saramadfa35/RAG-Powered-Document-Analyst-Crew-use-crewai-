## Executive Summary
Nimbus operates under a hybrid-first remote work policy that will take effect in January 2025, categorizing employees into Remote-first, Hybrid, and On-site work modes. In terms of financial performance, the company reported a revenue of $8.4 million for Q3 2026, which is an 18.3% increase compared to $7.1 million in Q2 2026. This growth is largely attributed to the successful launch of NimbusSync Pro v4.2 and its SmartRoute Sync Engine feature.

## Details
Nimbus's remote work policy, effective January 2025, is designed to accommodate various employee needs through a hybrid-first approach. Employees are classified into three distinct work modes:

1. **Remote-first**: Employees in fully remote roles, primarily in Engineering and Customer Success, can work from anywhere within their contracted country. They must maintain a 4-hour daily overlap with their team's core timezone.
   
2. **Hybrid**: Employees living within 50km of an office hub (Austin, Lisbon, Amman) are expected to work in the office at least 2 days per week, with flexibility in choosing those days in coordination with their manager.

3. **On-site**: Certain roles, such as those in Facilities, Executive Assistance, and Hardware QA, require employees to be on-site 5 days a week due to the nature of their work.

For employees wishing to work remotely outside their contracted country, a Cross-Border Work Request must be submitted to People Operations at least 60 days in advance. Approval is not guaranteed and depends on legal and tax considerations related to entity registration in the target country. Additionally, all remote and hybrid employees receive a one-time stipend of $600 for home-office setup and a recurring monthly connectivity allowance of $40.

In terms of financial performance, Nimbus reported total revenue of $8.4 million for Q3 2026, which shows a significant increase from $7.1 million in Q2 2026, marking an 18.3% quarter-over-quarter growth. This increase is primarily attributed to the launch of NimbusSync Pro v4.2 and its SmartRoute Sync Engine feature, which drove a 27% rise in new Enterprise-tier contract signings, particularly from logistics customers in the EU and MENA regions.
