## Executive Summary
Nimbus operates a hybrid-first remote work policy effective January 2025, categorizing employees into Remote-first, Hybrid, and On-site work modes. In terms of financial performance, the company reported a revenue of $8.4 million for Q3 2026, which marks an 18.3% increase compared to $7.1 million in Q2 2026. This growth is attributed to the successful launch of NimbusSync Pro v4.2.

## Details
The company's remote work policy is structured around three classifications of work modes. Remote-first employees can work from anywhere within their contracted country and must maintain a 4-hour overlap with their team's core timezone. Hybrid employees, located within 50km of an office hub, are required to work in-office at least two days per week. On-site roles, such as those in Facilities and Hardware QA, necessitate a full-time presence in the office.

Additionally, employees wishing to work remotely from outside their hired country must submit a Cross-Border Work Request to People Operations with a 60-day notice, and approval is contingent upon legal and tax considerations. All remote and hybrid employees receive a one-time $600 home-office stipend and a recurring $40 monthly connectivity allowance.

Financially, Nimbus experienced a significant increase in revenue in Q3 2026, totaling $8.4 million, up from $7.1 million in Q2 2026. This increase of 18.3% is primarily linked to the launch of NimbusSync Pro v4.2 and its new SmartRoute Sync Engine feature, which notably boosted new Enterprise-tier contract signings by 27%, especially among logistics customers in the EU and MENA regions.
